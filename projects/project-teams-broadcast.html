SCHOOL PROJECT

Teams Broadcast System

Power Platform solution that eliminates the manual hassle of broadcasting messages to 20+ Microsoft Teams channels. Built for teachers, students, and organizational leaders.

The Problem

Sending the same message to 20+ Teams channels manually is tedious. You have to open each one, copy-paste, and hope you didn't forget any. There's no way to track what was sent where.

The Solution

This tool lets you select all channels at once, write your message once, and broadcast everywhere with one click. Full logs show exactly what was sent where.

Built for teachers managing hundreds of class channels, students uploading to multiple courses, project leaders coordinating across departments, and directors sending company-wide announcements. Anyone who needs consistent messaging across multiple Teams channels.

How It's Built

Power Apps handles the UI. Select channels, write messages, see real-time status. The interface blocks you from sending twice and shows clear feedback while broadcasting.

Power Automate has two flows working together. The first flow automatically fetches all your Teams and channels when you open the app or click "Refresh Channels". The second flow handles the actual sending, posting your message to each selected Teams channel and returning success or failure for each one.

Dataverse stores the logs. Search through broadcast history anytime. Every message is tracked with timestamps, sender info, and per-channel delivery status.

Main broadcast interface: channel selection, message composition, and real-time sending status. Uses real Teams with test channels to avoid spamming school channels during development.

What I Learned

Power Automate's premium features required creative workarounds. The learning curve was steep, but I focused on proper architecture: separate UI/logic/data layers, proper state management (sending/success/error), and audit trails from day one.

Building this taught me that low-code doesn't mean low-quality. With the right design patterns, you can build production-grade tools. State management is critical for async operations. Users need clear feedback at every step, or they get confused and frustrated.

The hardest part wasn't the code. It was understanding Power Platform's conventions and working around its limitations without making the UX suffer.
